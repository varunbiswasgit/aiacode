◆ How to Play ◆

◆ Objective

Claim as many triangles as possible by connecting dots on the board. The player with the most completed triangles when no more legal moves remain wins.

◆ Setup

  • The board starts with 24 randomly placed dots.
  • Two players alternate turns: Player 1 (Blue) and Player 2 (Red).
  • No lines or triangles exist at the start.

◆ Dice Mode (Default)

  1. Roll the Dice — press Roll Dice to get 1–6 moves for your turn.
  2. Draw Lines — click any two dots to connect them. Each line uses one move.
  3. Turn passes automatically when moves run out.

◆ Classic Mode (Dice Off)

  • Toggle Dice: OFF to play in Classic mode — no dice required.
  • Each player draws exactly 1 line per turn.
  • Bonus turn: completing a triangle earns you one more move immediately — just like the classic Dots & Boxes game.
  • Your turn continues as long as each move completes at least one triangle. When a move closes no triangle, the turn passes.
  • No Roll Dice button needed — just click two dots to draw.

◆ Legal Moves

  • No duplicates — the same line cannot be drawn twice.
  • No crossings — the new line must not intersect any existing line (except at shared endpoints).
  • No passing through dots — the line must not pass over any dot that is not one of its two endpoints.

◆ Claiming Triangles

  • When your line closes a triangle — three dots all connected — that triangle is automatically claimed and filled with your color.
  • A triangle is valid only if no other dot sits inside it.
  • Multiple triangles can be completed in one move.

◆ Ending the Game

  • The game ends when no legal line can be drawn anywhere on the board.
  • Either player may press Finish to end the game and reveal results.
  • The player with more triangles wins. Equal counts result in a draw.

◆ Other Controls

  • New Game — resets the board, scores, and dots entirely.
  • Clicking a selected dot deselects it.
